How TruCite Works

1. Output Interception

TruCite integrates downstream of LLMs, RAG systems, and AI agents, evaluating generated output before it enters enterprise workflows.

TruCite analyzes linguistic certainty, numeric claims, volatility signals, internal consistency, and evidence presence to assess structural reliability.

Reliability signals are evaluated against enterprise policy rules to produce a machine-enforceable decision: ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK.

TruCite returns both a structured decision signal for downstream systems and an immutable cryptographic fingerprint for audit, compliance, and governance tracking.

Policy Notes

      

        

          
Execution Decision (MVP)

          

            The current MVP produces an execution decision using lightweight integrity checks
            (structure, uncertainty cues, and evidence presence). Enterprise deployments extend this with
            domain-specific policy profiles, evidence adapters, and organization-level thresholds aligned to regulated workflows.
          

        


        

          
Conservative Guardrails

          

            TruCite defaults to fail-safe gating for high-liability outputs — numeric claims,
            clinical/legal assertions, and rapidly changing real-world facts — issuing REVIEW
            or BLOCK unless an acceptable evidence requirement is satisfied.
          

        


        

          
Decision Support, Not Truth Engine

          

            TruCite is not a “truth model.” It is an execution governance layer that outputs
            structured decisions (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) with machine-readable rationale so
            humans and downstream systems can enforce policy consistently.
          

        


        

          
Time-Sensitive Knowledge Safeguards

          

            Claims that depend on fast-changing real-world conditions (leadership roles, prices, regulations,
            market status) are treated as volatile and gated unless supporting evidence is present
            or a verified source requirement is satisfied.
          

        


        

          
Enterprise Audit Readiness

          

            TruCite outputs execution-bound decision artifacts — policy decisions
            (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK), evidence requirements, and cryptographic fingerprints —
            enabling enterprises to maintain audit trails, demonstrate enforcement, and reconstruct decision pathways
            when required.
          

        

      

    


    
    

      
Global + Multilingual Deployment


      

        

          
Language-Agnostic Risk Infrastructure

          

            TruCite is designed as language-agnostic reliability infrastructure. While the MVP emphasizes
            English-first signal analysis, enterprise deployments extend verification through modular
            multilingual signal libraries tailored to regional and regulatory contexts.
          

        


        

          
Why This Matters in High-Liability Markets

          

            In healthcare, legal, government, and financial workflows, critical decisions often occur in
            regional languages. TruCite supports global rollout by adding localized signal packs
            (confidence markers, hedging terms, domain-specific risk cues) while maintaining consistent
            audit outputs and policy enforcement logic.
          

        


        

          
Policy Gating Works Across Languages

          

            Structural risk signals such as numeric claims, certainty patterns, and volatile real-world facts
            are evaluated independently of model vendor or language. This allows TruCite to apply consistent
            ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK policy decisions across global deployments.
          

        


        

          
Enterprise Roadmap

          

            Planned enhancements include multilingual evidence parsing, language-aware volatility detection,
            and policy profiles aligned with country-specific compliance and risk standards.

FAQ's


      

        

          What does the TruCite symbol mean?
          

            The symbol is inspired in part by the Egyptian concept of Ma’at — truth and balance —
            where a feather is weighed against the heart. The golden feather represents truth,
            the blue orb represents signal, and the curved arc represents the
            verification layer weighing evidence before a score is rendered.
          

        


        

          Is TruCite a security gateway?
          

            No. TruCite does not encrypt data, manage identity, or replace confidential computing infrastructure.
            Those layers protect confidentiality and access control.
            


            TruCite operates at the decision layer — evaluating AI output reliability before it triggers downstream action.
          

        


        

        

          How is TruCite different from “guardrails” or moderation APIs?
          

            Guardrails filter or steer model outputs. TruCite produces deterministic execution decisions with audit-grade metadata (policy hash, event ID, audit fingerprint) so high-liability workflows can gate downstream actions in a machine-enforceable way.
          

        


        

          Can an enterprise override a BLOCK for treasury or payments?
          

            Not in MVP. In high-risk payment flows, TruCite is designed to block execution unless the workflow contains
            verified human approval outside the model path.
          






  Does TruCite “enforce” actions directly?
  

    TruCite emits a machine-readable decision (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an
    execution_commit artifact (event_id, policy_hash, audit_fingerprint) that downstream systems can enforce.
    


    In production, enforcement happens via policy hooks (SDK / middleware / API gateway integration) controlled by the customer.
  






  What’s next after the MVP?
  

    Two expansions:
    


    1) Enforcement Hooks — drop-in middleware + SDKs (LLM apps, agent orchestration, RAG pipelines)
    that block or route based on TruCite decisions.
    


    2) Audit Ledger — enterprise-grade traceability (immutable logs, retention policies, exportable compliance reports).
  




        

          How is TruCite different from OpenEvidence?
          

            OpenEvidence and similar tools focus on retrieval and citations.
            TruCite is a verification and policy layer: it scores the output itself and returns
            an enforceable gate action that downstream systems can use to control AI-driven workflows.
          

        


        


          

          Does TruCite support non-English or regional languages?
          

            TruCite is designed as language-agnostic reliability infrastructure. The MVP demo emphasizes
            English-first signal analysis, while enterprise deployments extend verification through modular
            multilingual signal libraries tailored to regional and regulatory contexts.
            


            Structural risk signals — including numeric claims, certainty patterns, and volatile real-world facts —
            are evaluated independently of model vendor or language. This allows TruCite to apply consistent
            ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK policy decisions across global and regional-language AI deployments.
            


            Planned enterprise enhancements include multilingual evidence parsing, language-aware volatility detection,
            and country-specific policy profiles aligned with local compliance standards.
          

        


        
        

          Why can’t model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) do this?
          

            Model providers optimize for generation quality—not independent execution control.
            


            TruCite is designed to be model-agnostic and operate outside the model boundary,
            ensuring that execution decisions are not influenced by the same system generating the output.
            


            In regulated environments, execution validation must be independent, auditable, and enforceable—
            not embedded within the model itself.
          

        


        
        

          Is TruCite just a fact-checking or citation tool?
          

            No. TruCite does not aim to improve or rewrite AI output.
            


            It evaluates whether the output is safe to execute in a real-world context.
            


            The system issues a deterministic ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK decision based on evidence sufficiency,
            volatility, and execution risk—not just factual correctness.
          

        


        
        

          What happens if TruCite is wrong?
          

            TruCite does not execute actions—it issues a decision signal for downstream systems to enforce.
            


            In REVIEW scenarios, human oversight is expected before execution.
            


            The system is designed to be conservative in high-liability contexts,
            prioritizing prevention of unsafe execution over permissiveness.
            


            All decisions are audit-bound with an execution commit, enabling traceability and review.
          

        


        

          Does TruCite slow down real-time workflows?
          

            TruCite operates at millisecond-scale latency and is designed for real-time decisioning.
            


            It is deployed inline or at the API gateway layer, ensuring minimal impact on system performance
            while adding a critical execution checkpoint.
          

        


          

          Does TruCite “enforce” actions directly?
          

            TruCite emits a machine-readable decision (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an
            execution_commit artifact (event_id, policy_hash, audit_fingerprint) that downstream systems can enforce.
            


            In production, enforcement happens via policy hooks (SDK / middleware / API gateway integration) controlled by the customer.
          

        


        

          What’s next after the MVP?
          

            Next phases include enforcement hooks (middleware, SDKs, gateway policy adapters) and a full audit ledger for enterprise traceability.
          

        


        

          How is TruCite different from OpenEvidence?
          

            OpenEvidence and similar tools focus on retrieval and citations.
            TruCite is a runtime execution boundary that determines whether output is safe to act on.
          

        


        

          If RAG or data pipelines are already grounded, why is TruCite needed?
          

            Grounding improves input quality but does not guarantee execution safety.
            


            TruCite evaluates output at runtime — detecting missing evidence, unsupported claims,
            and policy violations before downstream systems act.
            


            It is the final execution decision layer, not a data pipeline component.
          

        


        

          Does TruCite store my content?
          

            In this public MVP demo, no. Enterprise deployments can support encrypted audit trails under strict retention controls.
