Where TruCite Deploys First

TruCite deploys first into high-liability workflows where AI output directly influences clinical, legal, financial, or operational decisions.

  • Legal AI Platforms — prevent fabricated case law, unsupported authority claims, or unverifiable legal output from reaching courts, clients, or regulators (bar sanctions, reputational exposure, liability risk).

  • Enterprise Copilots — stop policy-violating or evidence-insufficient actions before automated workflows trigger (operational breach, contractual risk, downstream failure).

Where TruCite Fits in the AI Stack

Upstream: AI Models & Agents

LLMs, RAG pipelines, and agent systems generate output intended for operational workflows.

TruCite: Independent Execution Gate

TruCite sits between generation and action. It inspects AI output at runtime and issues a machine-enforceable ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK decision, bound to an audit fingerprint and policy metadata, so downstream systems can block execution or require review.

It does not generate content. It governs whether content is safe to act on.

Downstream: Enterprise Execution Systems

Workflow orchestration, identity, and automation systems consume TruCite’s decision signal before executing AI-driven actions.

TruCite does not replace orchestration, governance, or security — it provides the execution decision boundary those systems can enforce.

Runtime Decision Boundary

The liability surface is not generation — it is the moment AI output enters workflows, automation, customer delivery, clinical guidance, legal reasoning, or financial action. TruCite gates that transition.

TruCite Is Not Model Eval, RAG, or “Guardrails”

Model Evals (Benchmarks)

Measure how models perform in general. Useful for research and selection — but they do not return a runtime enforcement decision inside enterprise workflows.

RAG / Retrieval

Improves grounding by fetching sources. But citations do not guarantee the output is coherent, safe, or action-ready — and retrieval alone does not gate what gets executed.

Safety / Red-Teaming

Tests models for jailbreaks and harmful behavior. Important — but mostly pre-deployment. Enterprises still need a runtime control layer when outputs hit real users and decisions.

TruCite (Runtime Decision Gate)

TruCite is a model-agnostic control layer that evaluates output risk in real time and returns: Score + ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK + audit fingerprint for downstream enforcement and compliance.

Important: TruCite is the reliability decision boundary at execution time — not an orchestration layer, not an agent governance platform, and not a monitoring dashboard.

How TruCite Works

1. Output Interception

TruCite integrates downstream of LLMs, RAG systems, and AI agents, evaluating generated output before it enters enterprise workflows.

2. Structural Reliability Analysis

TruCite analyzes linguistic certainty, numeric claims, volatility signals, internal consistency, and evidence presence to assess structural reliability.

3. Policy-Based Decisioning

Reliability signals are evaluated against enterprise policy rules to produce a machine-enforceable decision: ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK.

4. Enforcement Signal + Audit Fingerprint

TruCite returns both a structured decision signal for downstream systems and an immutable cryptographic fingerprint for audit, compliance, and governance tracking.

Why not let the model vendor do this?

Enterprises increasingly require separation between model generation and execution enforcement. A model vendor grading its own output does not create an independent accountability boundary.

Policy Notes

  


    

      
Execution Decision (MVP)

      

        The current MVP produces an execution decision using lightweight integrity checks
        (structure, uncertainty cues, and evidence presence). Production deployments extend this with
        domain policy profiles, evidence adapters, and organization-specific thresholds aligned to regulated workflows.
      

    


    

      
Conservative Guardrails

      

        TruCite defaults to fail-safe gating for high-liability outputs — numeric claims,
        clinical/legal assertions, and rapidly changing real-world facts — issuing REVIEW
        or BLOCK unless an acceptable evidence requirement is satisfied.
      

    


    

      
Decision Support, Not Truth Engine

      

        TruCite is not a “truth model.” It is an execution governance layer that outputs
        structured decisions (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) with machine-readable rationale so
        humans and downstream systems can enforce policy consistently.
      

    


    

      
Time-Sensitive Knowledge Safeguards

      

        Claims that depend on fast-changing real-world conditions are treated as volatile and gated unless
        supporting evidence is present or a verified source requirement is satisfied.
      

    


    

      
Enterprise Audit Readiness

      

        TruCite outputs execution-bound decision artifacts — policy decisions, evidence requirements, and cryptographic fingerprints —
        enabling enterprises to maintain audit trails, demonstrate enforcement, and reconstruct decision pathways when required.

FAQ


  


    

      What does the TruCite symbol mean?
      

        The symbol is inspired in part by the Egyptian concept of Ma’at — truth and balance —
        where a feather is weighed against the heart. The golden feather represents truth,
        the blue orb represents signal, and the curved arc represents the
        verification layer weighing evidence before a score is rendered.
      

    


    

      Is TruCite a model or a wrapper around LLMs?
      

        No. TruCite is a model-agnostic runtime decision layer that sits downstream of LLMs,
        RAG systems, and agent frameworks.
      

    


    

      Is this just fact-checking or citations?
      

        No. Citations can support grounding, but TruCite evaluates whether output is structurally reliable
        and safe enough to execute inside enterprise workflows.
      

    


    

      If RAG or data pipelines are grounded, why is TruCite needed?
      

        Retrieval adds sources. It does not determine whether the final output is coherent,
        policy-safe, sufficiently supported, or execution-ready.
      

    


    

      What makes TruCite different from model evaluations or benchmarks?
      

        Model evaluations measure performance in controlled settings. TruCite operates at runtime —
        issuing a decision at the moment output is about to trigger real-world action.
      

    


    

      Is TruCite part of the AI stack or a separate layer?
      

        TruCite is a separate execution layer — a control plane between AI output and enterprise action.
        It is not part of the model, RAG pipeline, or orchestration layer.
      

    


    

      Why is an independent execution layer necessary?
      

        If the model, orchestrator, or vendor also decides whether output is safe, it creates a structural conflict.
        TruCite provides an independent boundary so enterprises can enforce decisions without relying on the generator.
      

    


    

      Does TruCite replace guardrails or safety layers?
      

        No. Guardrails shape output. TruCite determines whether that output is safe enough to execute.
        It operates after generation, before action.
      

    


    

      Does TruCite execute actions?
      

        No. TruCite issues a decision signal and audit artifact.
        Customer-controlled systems enforce the boundary before downstream commit.
      

    


    

      Can an enterprise override a BLOCK decision?
      

        In high-risk workflows, overrides require explicit human authorization outside the model path.
        Enterprise versions support audit-bound override mechanisms tied to the original decision artifact.
      

    


    

      What happens if TruCite is wrong?
      

        TruCite is designed as a conservative runtime gate. In high-liability workflows,
        insufficient evidence or elevated risk defaults to REVIEW or BLOCK rather than unsafe ALLOW.
      

    


    

      Does this slow down systems?
      

        TruCite is designed for low-latency runtime evaluation suitable for enterprise workflows,
        typically operating within real-time decision thresholds.
      

    


    

      Is TruCite for consumers?
      

        No. TruCite is built for regulated enterprise workflows, not consumer AI usage.
