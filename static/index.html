Runtime Execution Reliability Control Plane for AI Systems

TruCite is a model-agnostic runtime execution reliability control plane that assigns a deterministic risk score to AI output before it triggers downstream action ‚Äî issuing a machine-enforceable ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK decision with an execution-bound audit fingerprint.

Architecturally independent of model vendors, orchestration layers, and security gateways, TruCite provides a neutral reliability signal across heterogeneous AI stacks.

As AI systems transition from generating content to initiating real-world actions, a runtime execution reliability boundary becomes foundational infrastructure.

Built for high-liability workflows (healthcare, legal, finance, government): evidence is required to reach ALLOW for numeric, volatile, or high-impact assertions.

Execution Reliability Is Not the Same as Security

Security protects what models see. TruCite protects what models do.

In high-liability workflows, correctness before action is not optional infrastructure.

Policy Notes

Conservative Guardrails

      

        TruCite applies conservative gating to reduce unsafe ALLOW outcomes for debunked claims,
        high-liability content, numeric assertions, and volatile real-world facts when evidence is absent.
      

    


    

      
Decision Support, Not Truth Engine

      

        TruCite does not determine factual truth. It provides structured reliability signals and policy-based
        decision outputs that support human oversight and downstream system enforcement.
      

    


    

      
Time-Sensitive Knowledge Safeguards

      

        Claims dependent on rapidly evolving real-world facts (e.g., leadership roles, market conditions,
        regulatory status) are flagged as volatile and gated unless supporting evidence signals are present.
      

    


    

      
Enterprise Audit Readiness

      

        TruCite outputs include structured scoring signals, policy decisions, and cryptographic fingerprints,
        enabling enterprises to maintain audit trails, demonstrate policy enforcement, and reconstruct
        decision pathways when required.

        What does the TruCite symbol mean?

        How is TruCite different from OpenEvidence?
        

          OpenEvidence and similar tools focus on retrieval and citations.
          TruCite is a verification and policy layer: it scores the output itself and returns
          an enforceable gate action that downstream systems can use to control AI-driven workflows.
        

      


      

        If our data pipeline is secure and governed, why do we still need TruCite?
        

          Secure data and governance ensure trusted inputs. But LLMs and agents transform that data into newly generated output,
          and that generation step introduces risk: hallucinated facts, overconfident language, misapplied guidelines, and
          reasoning errors ‚Äî even with perfect source documents.
          


          TruCite covers the post-generation risk layer by scoring output reliability and issuing a machine-enforceable decision
          (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an audit fingerprint for compliance, logging, and downstream enforcement.
        

      


      

  Does TruCite handle execution governance or authorization?

    It determines whether AI-generated output is safe to act on and returns
    a machine-enforceable decision signal.
    


    Execution governance ‚Äî including identity binding, authorization, and workflow control ‚Äî
    is handled by downstream orchestration or enterprise systems that consume TruCite‚Äôs decision output.
  

      


      

        Is this just ‚ÄúAI correcting AI‚Äù? Isn‚Äôt that risky?

          TruCite is model-agnostic. It focuses on output integrity and risk signals rather than internal vendor confidence scores.
          This allows TruCite to function as a stable verification and enforcement layer even as underlying models evolve.
