Policy Notes

  


    

      
Execution Decision (MVP)

       

        The current MVP produces an execution decision using lightweight integrity checks
        (structure, uncertainty cues, and evidence presence).
        Production deployments extend this with domain policy profiles, evidence adapters, and
        organization-specific thresholds aligned to regulated workflows.
       

    


    

      
Conservative Guardrails

      

       TruCite defaults to fail-safe gating for high-liability outputs ‚Äî numeric claims,
       clinical/legal assertions, and rapidly changing real-world facts ‚Äî issuing REVIEW
       or BLOCK unless an acceptable evidence requirement is satisfied.
      

    


    

      
Decision Support, Not Truth Engine

      

       TruCite is not a ‚Äútruth model.‚Äù It is an execution governance layer that outputs
       structured decisions (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) with machine-readable rationale so
       humans and downstream systems can enforce policy consistently.
      

    


    

      
Time-Sensitive Knowledge Safeguards

      

       Claims that depend on fast-changing real-world conditions (leadership roles, prices, regulations,
       market status) are treated as volatile and gated unless supporting evidence is present
       or a verified source requirement is satisfied.
      

    


    

      
Enterprise Audit Readiness

        

         TruCite outputs execution-bound decision artifacts ‚Äî policy decisions
         (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK), evidence requirements, and cryptographic fingerprints ‚Äî
         enabling enterprises to maintain audit trails, demonstrate enforcement, and reconstruct decision pathways
         when required.

        What does the TruCite symbol mean?
        

          The symbol is inspired in part by the Egyptian concept of Ma‚Äôat ‚Äî truth and balance ‚Äî
          where a feather is weighed against the heart. The golden feather represents truth,
          the blue orb represents signal, and the curved arc represents the
          verification layer weighing evidence before a score is rendered.
        

      


      

  Is TruCite a security gateway?
  

    No. TruCite does not encrypt data, manage identity, or replace confidential computing infrastructure.
    Those layers protect confidentiality and access control.
    


    TruCite operates at the decision layer ‚Äî evaluating AI output reliability before it triggers downstream action.
  




      

  How is TruCite different from ‚Äúguardrails‚Äù or moderation APIs?
  

    Guardrails filter or steer model outputs. TruCite produces deterministic execution decisions with audit-grade metadata (policy hash, event ID, audit fingerprint) so high-liability workflows can gate downstream actions in a machine-enforceable way.
  

      




  Does TruCite ‚Äúenforce‚Äù actions directly?
  

    TruCite emits a machine-readable decision (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an
    execution_commit artifact (event_id, policy_hash, audit_fingerprint) that downstream systems can enforce.
    


    In production, enforcement happens via policy hooks (SDK / middleware / API gateway integration) controlled by the customer.
  






  What‚Äôs next after the MVP?
  

    Two expansions:
    


    1) Enforcement Hooks ‚Äî drop-in middleware + SDKs (LLM apps, agent orchestration, RAG pipelines)
    that block or route based on TruCite decisions.
    


    2) Audit Ledger ‚Äî enterprise-grade traceability (immutable logs, retention policies, exportable compliance reports).
  




      

        How is TruCite different from OpenEvidence?
        

          OpenEvidence and similar tools focus on retrieval and citations.
          TruCite is a verification and policy layer: it scores the output itself and returns
          an enforceable gate action that downstream systems can use to control AI-driven workflows.
        

      


      

        If our data pipeline is secure and governed, why do we still need TruCite?
        

          Secure data and governance ensure trusted inputs. But LLMs and agents transform that data into newly generated output,
          and that generation step introduces risk: hallucinated facts, overconfident language, misapplied guidelines, and
          reasoning errors ‚Äî even with perfect source documents.
          


          TruCite covers the post-generation risk layer by scoring output reliability and issuing a machine-enforceable decision
          (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an audit fingerprint for compliance, logging, and downstream enforcement.
        

      


      

  Does TruCite handle execution governance or authorization?
  

    TruCite focuses on output reliability and decision governance.
    It determines whether AI-generated output is safe to act on and returns
    a machine-enforceable decision signal.
    


    Execution governance ‚Äî including identity binding, authorization, and workflow control ‚Äî
    is handled by downstream orchestration or enterprise systems that consume TruCite‚Äôs decision output.
  

      


      

        Is this just ‚ÄúAI correcting AI‚Äù? Isn‚Äôt that risky?
        

          TruCite does not replace your models or generate new content. It operates as an independent scoring and
          policy enforcement layer that supports human oversight and system-level safeguards.
        

      


      

        How is TruCite future-proof if models keep changing?
        

          TruCite is model-agnostic. It focuses on output integrity and risk signals rather than internal vendor confidence scores.
          This allows TruCite to function as a stable verification and enforcement layer even as underlying models evolve.
