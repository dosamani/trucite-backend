FAQ's


  


    

      What does the TruCite symbol mean?
      

        The symbol is inspired in part by the Egyptian concept of Ma‚Äôat ‚Äî truth and balance ‚Äî
        where a feather is weighed against the heart. The golden feather represents truth,
        the blue orb represents signal, and the curved arc represents the
        verification layer weighing evidence before a score is rendered.
      

    


    

      How is TruCite different from OpenEvidence?
      

        OpenEvidence and similar tools focus on retrieval and citations.
        TruCite is a verification and policy layer: it scores the output itself and returns
        an enforceable gate action that downstream systems can use to control AI-driven workflows.
      

    


    

      If our data pipeline is secure and governed, why do we still need TruCite?
      

        Secure data and governance ensure trusted inputs. But LLMs and agents transform that data into newly generated output,
        and that generation step introduces risk: hallucinated facts, overconfident language, misapplied guidelines, and
        reasoning errors ‚Äî even with perfect source documents.
        


        TruCite covers the post-generation risk layer by scoring output reliability and issuing a machine-enforceable decision
        (ALLOW / REVIEW / BLOCK) plus an audit fingerprint for compliance, logging, and downstream enforcement.
      

    


    

      Is this just ‚ÄúAI correcting AI‚Äù? Isn‚Äôt that risky?
      

        TruCite does not replace your models or generate new content. It operates as an independent scoring and
        policy enforcement layer that supports human oversight and system-level safeguards.
      

    


    

      How is TruCite future-proof if models keep changing?
      

        TruCite is model-agnostic. It focuses on output integrity and risk signals rather than internal vendor confidence scores.
        This allows TruCite to function as a stable verification and enforcement layer even as underlying models evolve.
      

    


    

      Does TruCite support non-English or regional languages?
      

        TruCite is designed as language-agnostic risk infrastructure. Early deployments emphasize English-first signal analysis,
        with multilingual expansion planned for global enterprise workflows.
        


        Even in multilingual scenarios, TruCite‚Äôs policy gating remains consistent. Structural risk signals,
        certainty vs uncertainty patterns, numeric claim handling, and volatility detection are evaluated
        independent of model vendor or language.
      

    


    

      Does TruCite store my content?
      

        In this public MVP demo, no. Enterprise deployments can support encrypted audit trails under strict retention controls.
